Umar ibn al-Khattab (R.A)<br>

Date of Birth: 584 CE<br>
Date of Death: 644 CE<br>
City of Residence: Medina, Saudi Arabia<br>
Place of Birth: Makkah, Saudi Arabia<br>
<br>
Umar ibn al-Khattab, renowned for his just and effective leadership, served as the second caliph of the Rashidun 
Caliphate. His strategic governance led to the expansion of the Islamic state into the Sassanian and Byzantine empires.
Noted for his emphasis on justice and administrative reforms, Umar's leadership was characterized by the establishment 
of a comprehensive administrative system, including the introduction of the Islamic calendar. His dedication to the 
welfare of the Muslim community, coupled with his devotion to upholding Islamic principles, earned him respect and 
admiration among both contemporaries and later generations.
