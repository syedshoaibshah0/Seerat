Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas (R.A) <br>

Date of Birth: 595 CE<br>
Date of Death: 674 CE<br>
City of Residence: Medina, Saudi Arabia<br>
Place of Birth: Makkah, Saudi Arabia<br>
<br>
Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas, renowned for his military leadership during the early Islamic conquests, was among the first 
converts to Islam. His participation in pivotal battles, such as the Battle of Badr and the Battle of Uhud, showcased 
his bravery and dedication to the cause of Islam. Sa'd's unwavering commitment to the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad 
(PBUH) and his crucial role in the expansion of the Islamic empire illustrate his significant contributions to the 
establishment and consolidation of the early Muslim community.
